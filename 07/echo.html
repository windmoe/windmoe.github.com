Mademoiselle Ma, the Model Teacher of JNU

By Xu Yifei College of Foreign Studies

Miss Lihong Ma, born in 1972 in Shanxi Province, joined College of Foreign Studies of Jinan University in 1999 with her double M.A. of French Literature and Education and Language Science. In 2010, she got her Ph.D. of French Literature and Comparative Literature.

As a model teacher, Miss Ma not only focuses on her professional knowledge and teaching skills but also her own moral characters. Having dedicated 14 years to French teaching, she has been sparing no effort on teaching every class with earnest attitude.

The establishment of French Department was never easy. Miss Ma was the only French teacher when she started her teaching career in Jinan University, so she had to teach all the students who learned French in the whole university. As an elite of the department, she contributed a lot to tutoring other new teachers.

In 2010, Miss Ma took on a mission of establishing French Department under the condition of there being only 3 French teachers. Out of the unbreakable determination, she had done so much work in order to make it happen. Eventually, the application of the establishment of French Department had been approved by Ministry of Education.

From the perspective of teaching abilities, Miss Ma keeps updating her knowledge about pedagogical theories and methods. She even sacrificed her own time for further studies and put what she had learned into practice, beneficial greatly to the students. As for her academic career, some of her articles were also published on domestic or foreign journals, some of which rank really high internationally, like Journal of Sociology.

Miss Ma also has a very close relationship with her students instead of merely being an absolute authority in class. Her care and inspiration for her students is the reason why her students always treat her as a family member.

JNU Helper---Help You, Help Myself

By Zhou Yige College of Foreign Studies

Founded in Oct, 2012, JNUHelper is an English tutoring volunteer service team from the College of Foreign Studies. Consistent with the value of “one for all, all for one”, it is aimed at assisting staff of businesses and organizations to improve their professional English.

Since its foundation, under the guidance of teachers, JNU Helper has developed into an experienced team of 15 key members together with more than 60 active members. At present, our team has four active subscribers respectively responsible for Women’s Hockey Team of Guangdong Province, TESCO, Primary School and Internal Advocacy Group. We welcome interested additions.

So far, JNUHelper has successfully implemented the following activities:

1. Oral English training for students in night classes in Jinan University;

2. Professional English training at the Niuting Roast Meat Shop of China Plaza;

3. Vocational English training for the Women's Hockey Team of Guangdong Province;

4. Tutoring students at Longtao Primary School;

5. Workplace English schooling for Tesco.

After the English training activities above, beneficiaries of our services respond with gratitude. They find a practical application for their every day life.

If you currently attend Jinan University, JNU Helper is absolutely the best means for you to serve others while also improving your English proficiency. We are now recruiting volunteers. If you are sincere, warmhearted and enthusiastic, come on! Don't hesitate to join us!

Dreams Sail a Long Voyage---2013 JNU English Speaking Contest

By Gao Yitong College of Foreign Studies

The 2013 English Speaking Contest final of Jinan University concluded successfully on May 16th. It attracted hundreds of audiences, most of whom are students from various majors.

A speech was given by Gongqi, the dean of College of Foreign Studies, to show his high expectation as well as encouragement to these contestants. In his speech, the contest was undoubtedly an excellent platform to create English atmosphere among students. Furthermore, he emphasized the great importance of reading laying a solid foundation for further improving.

The contestants gave a prepared speech on the topic of “Dream and Reality”and answered some relevant questions from distinguished judges, demonstrating their eloquence and excellent level of English. As what Tom, a foreign judge, said at last, it was not just a speaking contest but a channel to convey the thoughts from Chinese young colleges. He concluded that it was we that were the dream and future for China.

Finally, Mao Kuofei, a nonEnglish major student, stood out from nonEnglish major group. The champion of English major group was bestowed upon Wu Mengyun.

The Fifth Psychological Drama Preliminary Contest

By Lao Minhui, Paziliya Alimu College of Foreign Studies

The preliminary contest was held on May11th, 2013. There were a total of 48 teams taking part in the contest, 16 from the main campus, 25 from Zhuhai Campus, 2 from College of Chinese Language and Culture, and 7 from Shenzhen Tourism College.

The students of College of Foreign Studies this time came with two plays, both written by themselves. The freshmen's "The Aurora of Summer" was a story about the graduates' struggling to cope with their studies and find jobs, and their psychological changes under pressures. The juniors then drew their attention to the fresh students' attitude toward their new college life in “Right or Wrong to Live in My Own Way”. Both won favorable reviews for their humorous treatment and sincere performance.

We believe that through this competition, we will have a better psychological adaption to our college life and make it more colorful and exciting.

Carry Forward Lei Feng's Spirit and Promote Volunteerism

By Wang Jianyi College of Foreign Studies

This March witnessed the implementation of the activity – “ the month of promoting volunteering with Lei Feng as the role model” across the university. The Youth League Committee of our college launched a campaign on the theme of carrying forward Lei Feng's spirit and promoting volunteerism.

The twoweek long campaign featured a range of voluntary work organized by the YLC and the volunteer union of our college, including encouraging the students to find good deeds around us, visiting the nursing house, recycling secondhand books. Meanwhile, all kinds of new media such as microblog, QQ group and Wechat were all used to publize the activities and create a favorable atmosphere so as to galvanize everybody at out college into voluntary services.

Aside from mirroring our college's determination to practise the spirit of Lei Feng, the month of volunteering in March further boosts the cooperation and connection among all branches of our college's YLC. Our college will initiate more activities like this in the hope to contribute its share to building a harmonious campus.

2013 Semifinal Career Planning Contest

By Zhuo Minyi College of Foreign Studies

The semifinal of 2013 Career Planning Contest was successfully held on May 6th in the first-floor meeting room of the Second Arts Building, which aims to enhance students' comprehension about career planning.

Professor Gu Weifang delivered the opening speech to emphasize the importance of career planning and inspire the students to prepare themselves with skills and knowledge needed in society step by step from now on. Furthermore, positive attitude is necessary.

A comprehensive career plan involving self-aware-ness, professional cognition, specific implementation plan and adjustment enabled contestants to know how to achieve their career objectives. Five contestants won the applause of the judges and the spectators.

The top three awards were attained by Shan Zibing, Zhuo Minyi and Shao Ying. On behalf of our faculty, they will participate in the training camps for the final.
